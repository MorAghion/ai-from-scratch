WITNESS STATEMENT - CATHERINE REYNOLDS
Case #: BM-2024-089
Date: November 15, 2024
Interviewing Officer: Det. James Patterson
Location: Blackwood Manor, Drawing Room

STATEMENT:

My name is Catherine Marie Reynolds (née Blackwood), age 45. I am the ex-wife of Jonathan Blackwood (deceased). We divorced 3 years ago.

I was NOT supposed to be at the manor that evening. Jonathan called me at 6:45 PM and asked me to come over to discuss "a financial matter of mutual importance." He said it was urgent and couldn't wait.

TIMELINE OF EVENTS:

7:15 PM - Arrived at manor through the garden entrance (side gate) to avoid attention
7:30 PM - Met with Jonathan privately in his study for approximately 20 minutes
7:50 PM - Left study, Jonathan was alive and well, drinking from his port decanter
8:00 PM - Waited in the garden per Jonathan's request (he said he had family dinner but would call me back in)
9:30 PM - Still waiting, grew impatient, walked garden paths
10:20 PM - Jonathan texted me: "Need 30 more minutes, please wait"
10:35 PM - Decided to peek through study window to see if he was still busy
10:50 PM - Finally gave up and left through the side gate
11:10 PM - Received call from Robert Thorne about Jonathan's death, returned immediately

REGARDING MY PRESENCE:

Yes, I was wearing Jasmine perfume - it's my signature scent, I wear it every day. Yes, I have pearl jewelry. But I was NOT in the study during the time of death. I was IN THE GARDEN, as witnesses can confirm.

REGARDING THE INSURANCE POLICY:

The $2 million policy is irrelevant. Under the divorce decree, I only receive that if Jonathan dies of NATURAL CAUSES or accident. Murder voids the policy - the payout goes to his estate. So murdering him would actually cost me $2 million. The police can verify this with the insurance company.

CONTRADICTORY DETAILS:

**FIRST VERSION:** I never approached the study window.

**CORRECTED VERSION:** Fine, I admit I looked through the study window around 10:35 PM. But the curtains were partially drawn. I could see Jonathan at his desk, ALIVE, talking to someone. I couldn't see who. There was a decanter and two glasses on the desk - he wasn't alone. I left because I was angry he was meeting with someone else during "our" meeting time.

**FURTHER CORRECTION:** The person I saw might have been a reflection or shadow. When I think about it more carefully, maybe there was only one glass? It was dark and I was emotional.

MOTIVE CLARIFICATION:

Jonathan owed me $87,000 in back alimony. He kept stalling. But killing him means I get NOTHING - the alimony obligation dies with him. He was worth more to me alive and paying monthly than dead.

The "urgent financial matter" he wanted to discuss was increasing my alimony by $3,000/month. He had his lawyer draft papers. He was finally being reasonable. Why would I kill him right before getting what I wanted?

ALIBI WITNESSES:

- The taxi driver who dropped me off at 7:15 PM (Garden Gate entrance)
- Margaret the housekeeper saw me in the garden around 9:45 PM
- Security footage should show me never entering the house after 7:50 PM

ADDITIONAL INFORMATION:

When I looked through the window at 10:35 PM, I noticed something odd: there were muddy footprints on the windowsill OUTSIDE, leading AWAY from the window. Someone had climbed OUT, not in. The prints were fresh - one was still wet.

I saw Robert Thorne's car parked in the far lot around 10:00 PM when I walked the eastern path. Strange, because he claimed to be inside the whole time.

I found this in the garden near the study window around 10:40 PM: a small empty vial with residue. I picked it up without thinking, then panicked and dropped it back in the bushes. I should have reported it immediately. It looked like a prescription bottle.

The study window was ALREADY jimmied open when I looked through it - the lock was broken, hanging loose.
