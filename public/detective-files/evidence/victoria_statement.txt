WITNESS STATEMENT - VICTORIA BLACKWOOD
Case #: BM-2024-089
Date: November 15, 2024
Interviewing Officer: Det. Sarah Martinez
Location: Blackwood Manor, Study

STATEMENT:

My name is Victoria Anne Blackwood, age 28. I am the daughter of Jonathan Blackwood (deceased).

On the evening of November 14th, I arrived at the manor at approximately 7:30 PM for what was supposed to be a family dinner. I was wearing a navy blue evening gown and my grandmother's pearl necklace with matching earrings.

TIMELINE OF EVENTS:

7:30 PM - Arrived at manor, greeted father briefly in the foyer
8:00 PM - Dinner service began, tense conversation about my art gallery proposal
9:45 PM - Left dining room after argument with father about the loan denial
10:00 PM - Went to the library on the second floor to calm down
10:15 PM - **CORRECTION**: I actually went to the powder room first, then to my old bedroom upstairs to retrieve a book
10:30 PM - Was in the library reading
11:00 PM - Heard commotion, came downstairs
11:05 PM - Found father's body with others

REGARDING THE PEARL EARRING:

I was wearing both earrings during dinner. Multiple people can confirm this. However, I noticed one was missing when I checked in the mirror around 10:45 PM in the library. I thought I had lost it upstairs. I have no idea how it ended up in father's study - I did NOT enter that room that evening.

REGARDING THE NOTE:

I did NOT write any note signed "-V" to my father. Yes, I was angry about the gallery funding, but I would never threaten him. Someone is trying to frame me.

The handwriting should be analyzed - it's not mine.

CONTRADICTORY DETAIL:

When pressed, I admit I did briefly go to the study around 9:30 PM, before dinner ended, to retrieve a book I had left there days ago. The door was unlocked then. Father wasn't there. I was only in there for 30 seconds. That must be when I lost the earring, though I don't remember it falling off.

MOTIVE CLARIFICATION:

Yes, I desperately needed funding for my art gallery. The lease would be lost if I didn't secure $250,000 by December 1st. But my father and I had another meeting scheduled for next week with the accountant. There was still hope. Why would I kill him before that meeting?

ALIBI WITNESSES:

- Margaret (housekeeper) saw me go upstairs around 10:00 PM
- I passed Robert Thorne in the hallway around 10:20 PM (he was heading to the billiards room)

ADDITIONAL INFORMATION:

I saw Catherine Reynolds in the garden through the library window around 10:40 PM. She was walking quickly, almost running, toward the east wing. She kept looking back at the house.

I also noticed the security system panel was blinking red (disabled) when I came downstairs at 9:30 PM, which was unusual.

My father had been receiving threatening phone calls for the past two weeks. He wouldn't tell me who they were from, but I heard him arguing on the phone three days ago, saying "You'll get nothing until the will is finalized."

STATEMENT CONCLUDED: 11:47 PM
Signed: Victoria A. Blackwood
