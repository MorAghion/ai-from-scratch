CRIME SCENE REPORT - CASE #47892
Location: Blackwood Manor, 1847 Hillcrest Ridge
Date: November 15, 2025
Time of Discovery: 11:47 PM

VICTIM:
Jonathan Blackwood, 58, prominent art dealer and owner of Blackwood Manor. Found deceased in his study at approximately 11:45 PM. Cause of death appears to be poisoning - forensics detected traces of cyanide in a glass of vintage port wine on his desk. Time of death estimated between 10:30 PM and 11:15 PM.

CRIME SCENE DETAILS:
The victim was discovered slumped over his mahogany desk, a half-empty glass of 1963 Taylor Fladgate port beside him. The study door was locked from the inside, with the key still in the lock. A window behind the desk was found slightly ajar, curtains billowing in the cold November wind. The victim's personal safe stood open, though nothing appeared to be missing according to preliminary inventory.

Notable observations:
- A pearl earring was found beneath the victim's chair, not matching any jewelry worn by the victim
- Muddy footprints near the window, size 10 men's shoe, leading to and from the garden
- The decanter of port on the sideboard appeared undisturbed, suggesting the poisoned glass may have been brought to the victim
- A crumpled note in the wastebasket reading "We need to talk about what you've done. Meet me at midnight. -V"
- Fresh scratches on the window frame, possibly from forced entry
- A faint scent of jasmine perfume lingering in the room

SUSPECTS PRESENT AT DINNER PARTY:

1. VICTORIA BLACKWOOD (32, Female)
   Relationship: Victim's daughter from first marriage
   Statement: Claims she was in the library from 10:15 PM onwards, reading. Says she last saw her father at 9:45 PM when dinner concluded. Wearing a navy evening gownץ Appeared composed but eyes were red, possibly from crying. Has a history of arguments with victim over her inheritance and his refusal to fund her art gallery venture.

2. MARCUS CHEN (45, Male)
   Relationship: Victim's business partner for 12 years
   Statement: States he was in the billiard room with Robert from 10:00 PM until approximately 11:30 PM. Wearing a grey suit, size 11 shoes. Visibly nervous during questioning, hands trembling. Recently discovered that the victim had been selling forged paintings through their gallery, potentially implicating Marcus in the fraud.

3. CATHERINE REYNOLDS (51, Female)
   Relationship: Victim's ex-wife (divorced 8 years ago)
   Statement: Claims she stepped outside to the garden for fresh air from 10:20 PM to 10:50 PM, then retired to the guest room. Wearing a burgundy dress, pearl jewelry set and jasmine perfume. Remained calm during questioning. Still listed as beneficiary on one of victim's life insurance policies worth $2 million. Has been pressuring the victim to increase alimony payments.

4. ROBERT THORNE (38, Male)
   Relationship: Victim's personal attorney and estate executor
   Statement: Confirms he was playing billiards with Marcus from 10:00 PM until 11:30 PM. Wearing a black tuxedo, size 10 shoes. Appeared uncomfortable when asked about the victim's will. Recently drafted a new will for the victim that dramatically changed the distribution of the estate, though the will had not yet been signed.

ADDITIONAL NOTES:
- The household staff had been given the evening off after serving dinner
- The mansion's security system was disabled between 10:15 PM and 11:00 PM (time code logs show manual override from the main panel in the hallway)
- A set of garden shears was found on the back terrace, with fabric fibers caught in the blades matching expensive suit material
- The victim had made several large cash withdrawals in the weeks prior to his death
- A bottle of prescription sedatives prescribed to one of the suspects was found in the downstairs bathroom, recently refilled but now half-empty

The investigation remains ongoing. All suspects remain at the manor pending further questioning.
